I study Information Science and Government at Cornell University, and work with the Social Dynamics Lab here. I enjoy research (especially if it involves clever data usage or agent-based modeling), design, and writing on occasion.

I like to think about the game-theoretic, psychological, and societal barriers that prevent people from connecting and relating how they might want to politically, productively, and personally. Relevant social problems include collective action, isolation, and poverty.

I view software (sometimes decentralized) as a promising tool for lowering these barriers; I also have a mind towards policy.

I'm also interested in information dissemination: how knowledge engines and the public interact, misinformation and misinterpretation, memetics, and so forth. Relevant social problems include polarization and moral tribalism, poor civic engagement, and shoddy journalism.

I see good writing and visualization as a way to make unintelligible data coherent, dry topics fun, and difficult learning easy.
